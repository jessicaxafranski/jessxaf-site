CONTEXT

Americanas Marketplace is one of Brazil's largest e-commerce platforms. During my time there, I worked across two separate product teams, each solving a distinct problem in the seller experience. The two initiatives are presented independently below.

Initiative 01

Order Tracking

System migration  ·  Infrastructure  ·  Support reduction

Database Migration System Integration Data Analysis Stakeholder Management

THE PROBLEM

When I joined the Order Tracking team, sellers were contacting support in high volumes asking about their orders. But the problem wasn't a missing feature. The data feeding our tracking page was unreliable, because order information was scattered across multiple disconnected databases with no unified source of truth. Fixing this required going deeper than any UI change could reach.

01 Fragmented Data Sources

Order information lived across multiple systems with no synchronization, making real-time tracking technically impossible.

02 High Support Contact Rate

Sellers reached out repeatedly about order status because the data shown to them was stale or simply missing.

03 Operational Bottleneck

Internal teams were manually reconciling order states across systems, which didn't scale and slowed down every resolution.

MY APPROACH

The initial instinct from leadership was to redesign the seller dashboard. I pushed back on that. A better interface on top of unreliable data would make sellers more aware of the problem, not less. My first move was to work closely with engineering to map the full data pipeline — understanding exactly where information broke down and what a real migration would require. That diagnostic shaped every decision that followed.

01
Fix the data before touching the UI

I scoped the migration with engineering before any design work began. We mapped every data source, identified the highest-impact consolidation points, and phased the rollout so live orders were never at risk during the transition.

02
Use support tickets as the north star metric

Instead of measuring success by technical milestones alone, I tracked support contact rate as our primary signal. This kept the team focused on the outcome that mattered: sellers no longer needing to ask where their order was.

Initiative 02

Seller Onboarding

Activation  ·  SMB growth  ·  32,000+ sellers

Seller Activation User Research Segmentation Compliance UX Scrum

THE PROBLEM

The Onboarding team's challenge was different in nature, but just as visible in the data. Sellers were dropping off before activation, and when I looked at where it was happening, a clear pattern emerged: the flow had been designed for large enterprises with dedicated compliance teams. For SMBs — our biggest growth opportunity — it was simply the wrong experience.

01 Wrong Audience Assumed

The flow required compliance knowledge most SMBs didn't have, stalling them at the exact step where they needed the most support.

02 No Recovery Path

Sellers who got stuck had no visibility into what was blocking them or how to move forward. Support escalation was the only way out.

03 Slow Compliance Loop

Validation happened manually after submission, creating unpredictable delays and back-and-forth that eroded trust before sellers made their first sale.

MY APPROACH

I started by talking directly to sellers who had abandoned the process partway through. What I kept hearing was consistent: they wanted to activate, they just kept hitting walls with no explanation of what was wrong or what to do next. The product wasn't being honest with them about where they stood. That insight shaped everything.

01
Segment the flow by seller profile

One flow was failing two very different audiences. I mapped drop-off by segment and redesigned separate journeys for SMBs and enterprises. The SMB track removed steps that only applied to large businesses. Both conversion rates improved.

02
Move compliance validation inline

Instead of reviewing compliance after submission, I worked with engineering and legal to surface blockers in real time, inside the flow itself. Sellers fixed issues as they went, which cut approval time and eliminated the back-and-forth loop.

03
Add activation status transparency

Sellers who stalled needed to understand exactly where they stood and what to do next. I shipped a clear status view with explicit next steps, which reduced support escalations for stuck activations and gave sellers a concrete reason to return and complete the process.

STRATEGIC IMPACT

Both initiatives addressed the same underlying challenge from different angles: making the marketplace more reliable and easier to operate for sellers. One fixed the infrastructure that existing sellers depended on. The other opened the door for tens of thousands of new ones.

Scalable Tracking Infrastructure A unified database enabled real-time order visibility and became the foundation for future seller-facing features that weren't possible on the legacy system.
Marketplace Growth at Scale 32,000+ new activated sellers expanded supply-side depth across categories, directly strengthening marketplace liquidity.
Reduced Operational Risk Automated compliance checks removed a manual, error-prone step, reducing regulatory exposure while improving the seller experience at the same time.
